# Chemical Formula Formatting Test

This document demonstrates the improved chemical formula formatting in the notes section.

## Simple Chemical Formulas

- Water: H2O
- Carbon dioxide: CO2
- Sodium chloride: NaCl
- Methane: CH4
- Ammonia: NH3
- Oxygen: O2
- Hydrogen: H2
- Nitrogen: N2

## Complex Organic Compounds

- Glucose: C6H12O6
- Ethanol: C2H5OH
- Acetic acid: CH3COOH
- Benzene: C6H6
- Sucrose: C12H22O11
- Aspirin: C9H8O4
- Caffeine: C8H10N4O2

## Ionic Compounds

- Sodium hydroxide: NaOH
- Calcium carbonate: CaCO3
- Magnesium sulfate: MgSO4
- Potassium nitrate: KNO3
- Ammonium sulfate: (NH4)2SO4

## Charged Ions

- Sodium ion: Na+
- Chloride ion: Cl-
- Calcium ion: Ca2+
- Sulfate ion: SO4^2-
- Nitrate ion: NO3^-
- Ammonium ion: NH4+
- Hydroxide ion: OH^-

## Complex Formulas with Parentheses

- Calcium hydroxide: Ca(OH)2
- Aluminum sulfate: Al2(SO4)3
- Iron(III) oxide: Fe2O3
- Copper(II) sulfate: CuSO4
- Barium chloride: BaCl2

## Hydrates

- Copper(II) sulfate pentahydrate: CuSO4·5H2O
- Magnesium sulfate heptahydrate: MgSO4·7H2O
- Sodium carbonate decahydrate: Na2CO3·10H2O

## Common Chemical Reactions

- Photosynthesis: 6CO2 + 6H2O → C6H12O6 + 6O2
- Combustion of methane: CH4 + 2O2 → CO2 + 2H2O
- Neutralization: HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H2O

## Notes

All chemical formulas are automatically detected and formatted with:
- Proper subscripting for numbers (e.g., H₂O instead of H2O)
- Beautiful blue color scheme
- Subtle glow effect
- Background highlighting in paragraphs
- Support for charges with superscript notation
- Support for complex formulas with parentheses
- Support for hydrates with dot notation

The formatting is applied automatically - no special syntax needed!
